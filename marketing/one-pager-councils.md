# Access Compass for Councils and Authorities

## Help Your Local Businesses Become More Accessible

Access Compass is a self-guided accessibility assessment platform that helps businesses identify barriers, create action plans, and improve access for people with disability.

Councils use Access Compass to roll out structured accessibility programs across their LGA, without hiring consultants for every business.

---

## The Problem

- 5.5 million Australians live with disability (21.4%, ABS 2022)
- 75% of people with disability have walked away from a business due to access barriers
- Most businesses want to do the right thing but don't know where to start
- Individual consultancy assessments cost $5,000-$15,000 per site
- Free checklists exist but provide no guidance on how to fix issues

There is nothing between an expensive consultant and a generic checklist. Access Compass fills that gap.

---

## What Businesses Get

- **Tailored self-assessment** across 40 modules (parking, entry, signage, customer service, digital, events, and more)
- **Guided help content** explaining what each question means and how to answer it
- **Resource Hub** with practical solutions graded by effort level (quick wins through comprehensive upgrades)
- **Disability Inclusion Action Plan (DIAP)** auto-generated from assessment findings, with prioritised actions
- **Accessibility report** summarising strengths, gaps, and priority actions
- **Evidence library** for uploading photos and documents

---

## What Your Council Gets (Authority Portal)

- **Program management**: create accessibility programs, select which modules apply, set timelines
- **Business enrolment**: manual, self-enrol link, or CSV bulk upload
- **Aggregate dashboard**: see completion rates, progress over time, and per-program breakdowns across your LGA
- **Guidance notes**: attach council-specific guidance to standard assessment questions
- **Privacy by design**: councils see completion status and progress, not individual answers or evidence
- **Flexible funding models**: council-funded (grants), business-funded, or co-funded
