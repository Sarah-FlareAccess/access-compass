## PERSONA 1: COUNCIL / LOCAL GOVERNMENT

### Who You're Talking To
- **Primary:** Accessibility Officer, Disability Inclusion Officer, Community Development Manager
- **Secondary:** Tourism/Events Manager, Economic Development Manager
- **Decision maker:** Director of Community Services or equivalent (budget holder)
- **Influencer:** Mayor's office (if accessibility is a political priority)

### The Problem They Feel
- They know accessibility matters but have no scalable way to support local businesses
- Consultant-led programs are expensive and only reach 5-15 businesses per round
- They have DIAP obligations but limited visibility into how local businesses are performing
- They get complaints from residents with disabilities about local venues but lack data to act
- They want to demonstrate community leadership but "accessibility" feels hard to measure

### Core Pitch (30-second version)
"Access Compass lets you deploy a structured accessibility program to every business in your LGA. Businesses assess themselves with expert-designed guidance, build action plans, and improve over time. You get real-time visibility into how your community is tracking, with aggregate data for your own DIAP reporting. It scales to hundreds of businesses at a fraction of what a consultant-led program costs."

### Objection Handling

**"We already work with an accessibility consultant."**
"That's great, and we're not replacing that relationship. Most councils use consultants for their own buildings and strategic DIAP work. Access Compass extends your reach to the hundreds of local businesses that your consultant doesn't have time to visit individually. Some councils use us alongside their consultant, with the consultant focusing on high-priority venues and Access Compass supporting the broader business community."

**"Our businesses won't engage with a self-assessment tool."**
"That's the most common concern, and it's valid. That's why every Authority plan includes onboarding support: a kickoff webinar, email templates your team can send, and a getting started guide for businesses. Councils that actively promote the program see 60-70% engagement rates. We also offer facilitated programs where we run workshops and check-ins directly with businesses, if you want higher-touch support."

**"We don't have budget for this right now."**
"Understood. Two options: our Essentials tier starts at $4,900/year for up to 20 businesses, which is typically well under discretionary spending thresholds. Alternatively, we offer founding partner pricing for early-adopter councils (30-40% discount for the first year), which makes it even easier to trial. Would it help if I put together a proposal you could take to your director?"

**"How is this different from a checklist?"**
"A checklist tells you what to look at. Access Compass tells you what to look at, why it matters, how to check it, what good looks like, and what to do about it. Every question includes context, help content, and links to practical resources. The platform also builds a prioritised action plan automatically, tracks progress over time, and generates reports. It's the difference between a thermometer and a health monitoring system."

**"Can we pilot it with a small group first?"**
"Absolutely. That's what most councils do. Our Essentials tier supports up to 20 businesses, which is a perfect pilot size. Run it for 6 months, see the results, then expand. We're happy to offer founding partner pricing for the pilot."

### Demo Script (30 minutes)

**0-5 min: Discovery**
- "Tell me about your current accessibility initiatives."
- "How do you currently support local businesses with accessibility?"
- "What does success look like for this kind of program?"

**5-15 min: Platform Demo**
- Show a business going through discovery questions
- Show 2-3 assessment questions with help content
- Show the action plan / DIAP workspace
- Show the authority dashboard: business enrolment, progress tracking, aggregate view

**15-20 min: Program Support**
- Walk through what's included: onboarding, templates, webinar, getting started guide
- Mention facilitation options: "If you want us more hands-on, we offer workshops and check-ins"
- Show a sample aggregate report

**20-25 min: Pricing**
- "Based on what you've told me, [Tier] would be the best fit because [reason]."
- Present 2 options: platform-only and platform + facilitation
- Mention founding partner pricing if applicable

**25-30 min: Next Steps**
- "Would you like me to put together a formal proposal?"
- "Who else would need to be involved in this decision?"
- "What's your timeline for this kind of initiative?"

## PERSONA 3: VENUE OPERATOR / PROCUREMENT PARTNER

### Who You're Talking To
- **Primary:** Head of Operations, Facilities Manager, Risk/Compliance Manager
- **Secondary:** CEO (smaller operators), Sustainability/ESG Manager (larger ones)
- **Examples:** MCG, MCEC, Scentre Group/Westfield, Dexus, Lendlease, shopping centres, airport operators

### The Problem They Feel
- They have tenants, concessionaires, or suppliers who need to meet accessibility standards
- They're liable for the overall venue experience but can't control every tenant/operator
- They face reputational risk if a tenant venue is inaccessible
- DDA complaints against the venue reflect on the operator regardless of which tenant caused the issue
- They need a way to demonstrate due diligence across their portfolio

### Core Pitch
"Access Compass lets you roll out structured accessibility assessment across every tenant, concessionaire, or operator in your venue portfolio. Each business assesses themselves with expert guidance, and you get aggregate visibility into compliance status across all sites. It's how you demonstrate due diligence and lift the standard across your entire operation."

## PERSONA 4: INDIVIDUAL BUSINESS

### Who You're Talking To
- **Primary:** Business owner, operations manager, marketing manager
- **Business types:** Hotels, restaurants, cafes, retail shops, gyms, pools, tourism operators, event venues, medical clinics, community centres

### The Problem They Feel
- They want to be accessible but don't know where to start
- Hiring an accessibility consultant feels expensive and intimidating
- They've had a complaint (or fear one) and don't know how to respond
- They see competitors marketing accessibility and feel behind
- They have a grant application that requires accessibility evidence

### Core Pitch (varies by trigger)

**Trigger: "I want to do better but don't know where to start"**
"Access Compass walks you through every part of your customer journey, from your website to your front door to your customer service. You answer guided questions at your own pace, and it builds a prioritised action plan telling you exactly what to fix first, with practical resources showing you how."

**Trigger: "I've had a complaint"**
"Access Compass helps you identify every accessibility gap in your venue and operations, not just the one that triggered the complaint. You'll have a structured action plan and documented evidence that you're actively improving. That's the strongest response to any complaint: demonstrable action."

**Trigger: "I need this for a grant application"**
"Access Compass generates a Disability Inclusion Action Plan (DIAP) directly from your assessment. That's exactly what most grant programs require. You assess your venue, the platform builds the DIAP, and you export it for your application."

**Trigger: "I want to attract more customers"**
"One in five Australians has a disability. Their families and friends choose venues based on accessibility. Access Compass helps you identify and fix the barriers that are turning away customers you don't even know you're losing."

## PERSONA 5: INSURER

### Who You're Talking To
- **Primary:** Head of ESG/Sustainability, Head of Partnerships, Innovation/InsurTech team
- **NOT:** Sales team or underwriting (too operational, need strategic entry point)

### The Problem They Feel
- They have zero structured data on accessibility risk in their commercial portfolio
- Public liability claims related to accessibility (slip/trip/fall, DDA complaints) are a known but unmeasured risk category
- ESG reporting increasingly requires disability/inclusion metrics
- They want to differentiate their commercial insurance products but "accessibility" hasn't been productised

### LinkedIn Message Template (First Touch)

"Hi [Name], I lead Access Compass, a platform that helps Australian customer-facing businesses assess and improve their accessibility. We're building the only structured, ongoing accessibility compliance dataset in the market. I'd love to explore whether this data could inform risk assessment or create a co-branded offering for [Insurer]'s commercial policyholders. Happy to share more if this is of interest."

### Meeting Pitch (15 minutes)

**Context (2 min):**
"One in five Australians has a disability. Every customer-facing business has accessibility exposure, from physical barriers to service gaps. But right now, insurers have no signal for which businesses are managing this risk and which aren't. Access Compass changes that."

**The data (5 min):**
"We have [X] businesses on the platform, across [Y] industries. Each business has a structured assessment covering [35+] areas of their customer journey. We track completion, action plan progress, and re-assessment improvement over time. This gives us a unique risk signal: businesses that are actively managing accessibility risk vs. those that aren't."

**The opportunity (5 min):**
"There are three ways we could work together:
1. Premium discount: Access Compass Assessed businesses get X% off public liability. Reduces claims, differentiates your product.
2. Data integration: We provide an API or report showing which policyholders are assessed. You factor it into underwriting.
3. Co-branded product: [Insurer] offers Access Compass to commercial policyholders as a risk management tool. We handle delivery, you get the risk reduction."

**Ask (3 min):**
"I'm not expecting a decision today. I'd love to understand who else should be in the room and what a pilot exploration would look like on your side."

## PERSONA 8: ENTERPRISE VENUE OPERATOR

### Who You're Talking To
- **Primary:** Head of Operations, Facilities Manager, Risk/Compliance Manager, ESG/Sustainability Manager
- **Examples:** Scentre Group (Westfield, 42 centres), Dexus (100+ properties), Lendlease, AMP Capital, airport operators (Sydney Airport, Melbourne Airport), stadium groups (MCG, Marvel Stadium, Optus Stadium)
- **Decision maker:** COO, Head of Property Operations, or Group Risk Manager

### The Problem They Feel
- DDA liability sits with the venue operator regardless of which tenant caused the issue
- They manage hundreds of tenants across multiple sites with no standardised accessibility baseline
- Insurance premiums and legal exposure increase with every undocumented accessibility gap
- ESG reporting now requires demonstrable disability/inclusion metrics
- One high-profile complaint or social media incident at one venue reflects on the entire brand

### Core Pitch
"Access Compass lets you deploy a standardised accessibility assessment across every tenant and operator in your portfolio. Each business self-assesses with expert guidance, you get aggregate compliance visibility across all sites, and you build a documented due diligence trail. One platform, hundreds of tenants, real-time risk visibility."

### Why This Is a $10M Lever
- One Scentre Group deal (42 centres, hundreds of tenants per centre) could be $100K-$500K
- Tenant compliance can be written into lease agreements: "tenants must complete Access Compass assessment annually"
- Portfolio-wide data creates the aggregate dataset insurers want to see
- These operators have procurement budgets that dwarf council budgets

### How to Approach
- **Channel:** Direct to Head of Operations or Risk/Compliance. LinkedIn or warm introduction through property industry contacts.
- **Timing:** After 3+ council case studies and at least one venue case study. These operators want proof at scale.
- **Entry point:** Pitch a pilot at one centre or property (10-20 tenants). Prove it works, then expand portfolio-wide.
- **Pricing:** Authority Pro or Enterprise tier. $15K-$50K+ per year depending on portfolio size. Custom scoping required.
