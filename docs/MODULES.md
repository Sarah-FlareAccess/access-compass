# Access Compass - Module Reference

Access Compass includes 17 accessibility modules organized across 4 customer journey phases. Each module contains foundation questions for quick assessments and detailed questions for comprehensive reviews.

## Module Details

### B1: Pre-visit Information

Helps businesses understand how they communicate accessibility information before customers arrive.

**Key questions:**
- Do you have accessibility information available for customers before they visit?
- Can customers contact you in a variety of ways to ask accessibility questions?
- Do staff know how to confidently respond to accessibility enquiries?

---

### B4.1: Website Basics

Covers fundamental website accessibility for all visitors.

**Key questions:**
- Can all website content be accessed using only a keyboard?
- Do all images have alternative text descriptions?
- Is there good colour contrast between text and background?
- Are forms properly labelled for screen readers?

---

### B4.2: Booking Systems and Forms

Focuses on accessible online booking and form completion.

**Key questions:**
- Can customers complete bookings without using a mouse?
- Do booking forms allow customers to request accessibility support?
- Is there an alternative way to book if the online system is difficult?

---

### B4.3: Video and Social Media

Addresses accessibility of multimedia content.

**Key questions:**
- Do your videos have captions or subtitles?
- Are transcripts available for audio or video content?
- Do you include image descriptions on social media?
- Do you avoid flashing content that could affect people with epilepsy?

---

### A1: Arrival, Parking and Drop-off

Covers the customer journey from arrival to the entrance.

**Key questions:**
- Do you have designated accessible parking spaces?
- Is there a drop-off zone near the entrance?
- Is the path from parking smooth, level, and wide enough?
- Is the parking area well-lit?

---

### A2: Entry and Doors

Focuses on building entrance accessibility.

**Key questions:**
- Is your main entrance step-free?
- Is the entrance door wide enough for wheelchair users (850mm+)?
- Are entrance doors easy to open?
- Is alternative accessible entrance clearly signed?

---

### A3a: Paths and Aisles

Covers internal circulation and movement.

**Key questions:**
- Are circulation routes wide enough (1.2m+)?
- Are pathways clear of obstacles and trip hazards?
- Are floor surfaces stable, firm, and slip-resistant?
- Is there step-free access between levels?

---

### A3b: Queues and Busy Times

Addresses queue management and crowded periods.

**Key questions:**
- Is seating available for customers who cannot stand while queuing?
- Do staff know how to offer priority service?
- Do you share information about busy times?
- Is there enough space for wheelchair users in queuing areas?

---

### A4: Seating, Furniture and Layout

Covers physical comfort and space usability.

**Key questions:**
- Is there a variety of seating options for different needs?
- Are service counters at an accessible height?
- Can furniture be rearranged for wheelchair users?
- Is there clear space for wheelchairs alongside companions?

---

### A5: Toilets and Amenities

Focuses on accessible facilities.

**Key questions:**
- Do you have accessible toilets on site?
- Are accessible toilets clearly signed and easy to find?
- Is the accessible toilet kept clear of storage items?
- Are grab rails and emergency pull cords working?

---

### A6: Lighting, Sound and Sensory Environment

Addresses sensory aspects of your environment.

**Key questions:**
- Is lighting consistent and adequate throughout?
- Are there quiet times or spaces for noise-sensitive customers?
- Do you inform customers about sensory conditions?
- Is there good contrast between surfaces?

---

### B2: Signage and Wayfinding

Covers navigation and directional information.

**Key questions:**
- Is there directional signage throughout your space?
- Is signage at a readable height for all?
- Does signage use clear, simple language and symbols?
- Is there good contrast between sign text and background?

---

### B3: Menus and Printed Materials

Focuses on accessible printed information.

**Key questions:**
- Are materials available in large print?
- Is text easy to read with good contrast?
- Can staff read materials aloud if needed?
- Are digital versions available (QR codes, website)?

---

### C1: Customer Service and Staff Confidence

Covers staff training and customer interaction.

**Key questions:**
- Have staff received disability awareness training?
- Do staff know how to assist customers with assistance animals?
- Do staff feel confident communicating with customers who have different needs?
- Do staff understand they cannot refuse service to someone with an assistance animal?

---

### C2: Bookings, Payments and Flexibility

Addresses flexibility in transactions.

**Key questions:**
- Do you offer flexible booking/cancellation policies?
- Can customers pay using a variety of methods?
- Are payment terminals at an accessible height?
- Do you offer companion/carer tickets free or reduced?

---

### A7: Safety and Emergencies

Covers emergency procedures for everyone.

**Key questions:**
- Do emergency procedures include provisions for people with disabilities?
- Are emergency alarms both audible and visual?
- Do staff know how to assist during emergencies?
- Are evacuation routes accessible for wheelchair users?
- Is there a refuge area for people who cannot use stairs?

---

### C3: Learning from Your Customers

Focuses on feedback and continuous improvement.

**Key questions:**
- Do you actively invite accessibility feedback?
- Can customers provide feedback in multiple formats?
- Is there a clear process for handling accessibility complaints?
- Do you track feedback to identify patterns?

---

## Review Modes

### Pulse Check (Foundation)
- Quick overview of accessibility
- 5-6 questions per module
- ~10-15 minutes per module
- Best for: Initial assessment, time-limited reviews

### Deep Dive (Detailed)
- Comprehensive accessibility review
- All questions including detailed follow-ups
- ~18-25 minutes per module
- Best for: Thorough assessment, compliance preparation
