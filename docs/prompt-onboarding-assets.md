# Onboarding Assets Creation Prompt

Use this as a prompt when ready to create Authority program onboarding materials.

---

## Asset 1: Business Getting Started Guide (PDF, 2-4 pages)

**Audience:** Business owners/managers enrolled in a council accessibility program.

**Structure:**
1. **Welcome** - What Access Compass is, why your council has partnered with us, what this means for your business (2-3 sentences)
2. **What to expect** - You'll assess [X] areas of your business, at your own pace, and get a practical action plan
3. **How long it takes** - Pulse Check: ~2-3 hours total (can be done in short sessions). Deep Dive: ~5-8 hours total.
4. **Getting started** - Step-by-step: create account, complete discovery, start your first module
5. **Tips for success** - Walk your venue while answering. Take photos as you go. Don't overthink the answers. Flag anything you're unsure about.
6. **Support** - Where to get help (email, Resource Hub, council contact)

**Tone:** Friendly, practical, not corporate. Assume the reader has never done an accessibility assessment before.

**Design:** Use Access Compass brand colours. Include 2-3 screenshots of the platform (discovery, a question with help content, the action plan).

## Asset 2: Council Outreach Email Templates (Google Doc)

**Audience:** Council officers sending these to local businesses.

Create 6 email templates. Each should be:
- Under 200 words
- Written so the council can copy-paste with minimal editing
- Include [brackets] for council-specific details

**Template 1: Program Invitation**
- Subject: [Council Name] is launching an accessibility program for local businesses
- Body: What the program is, why it matters, what the business needs to do, link to sign up, deadline if applicable

**Template 2: Reminder to Start (1 week after invite)**
- Subject: Have you started your accessibility assessment yet?
- Body: Quick reminder, link, how long it takes, mention support available

**Template 3: Halfway Encouragement (sent when business is 50% through)**
- Subject: You're halfway there! Keep going.
- Body: Acknowledge progress, highlight what they'll get at the end (action plan, DIAP), encourage completion

**Template 4: Completion Congratulations**
- Subject: Congratulations! You've completed your accessibility assessment.
- Body: Celebrate, explain next steps (review action plan, start improvements, access Resource Hub), mention re-assessment in 6-12 months

**Template 5: Re-assessment Prompt (6 months after completion)**
- Subject: Time to re-assess: see how far you've come
- Body: It's been 6 months, re-assessment shows improvement, link, takes less time the second round

**Template 6: Program Wrap-Up Summary**
- Subject: [Program Name] results: here's what we achieved together
- Body: Aggregate stats (X businesses participated, Y% completed, Z actions identified), thank participants, what's next

## Asset 3: Kickoff Webinar Slide Deck (Google Slides, 15-20 slides)

**Audience:** Businesses enrolled in a council program, attending a kickoff webinar.

**Structure:**
1. Title slide: "[Council Name] Accessibility Program, Powered by Access Compass"
2. Welcome + housekeeping (mute, questions at end, recording)
3. Why accessibility matters (1 in 5 Australians, ageing population, legal obligations, business benefits)
4. What this program involves (assess, plan, improve, track)
5. What you'll assess (overview of module groups: before arrival, getting in, during visit, service, policy)
6. How it works: Discovery (screenshot)
7. How it works: Assessment questions (screenshot showing help content)
8. How it works: Action plan (screenshot)
9. How it works: Resource Hub (screenshot)
10. How long it takes + tips for success
11. What the council sees (aggregate data only, your individual answers are private)
12. Support available (getting started guide, email support, Resource Hub)
13. Live demo (switch to platform, walk through 2-3 questions)
14. Q&A
15. Next steps: create your account today, link, deadline if applicable
16. Thank you + contact details

**Tone:** Encouraging, practical, not lecturing. Emphasise "this is about progress, not perfection."

**Record the webinar.** Councils that prefer async can share the recording instead of running live sessions.

## Asset 4: Council Admin Quick Start Guide (PDF, 2 pages)

**Audience:** Council officers who will manage the Authority Portal.

**Structure:**
1. **Logging in** - How to access the Authority Portal
2. **Creating a program** - Step by step with screenshots
3. **Enrolling businesses** - Manual, self-enrol link, CSV upload
4. **Reading the dashboard** - What each metric means (enrolled, started, completed, completion %)
5. **Sending reminders** - How to use the email templates we provide
6. **Running reports** - How to generate and export aggregate reports
7. **Getting help** - Contact details for platform support

---

## Asset 5: One-Page Program Overview (PDF, 1 page)

**Audience:** Council internal stakeholders (directors, councillors) who need to approve the program.

**Structure:**
- **What:** A structured accessibility assessment and improvement program for local businesses
- **Why:** Support DIAP objectives, demonstrate community leadership, respond to accessibility complaints proactively, measurable outcomes for reporting
- **How:** Businesses self-assess using expert-designed platform, council tracks progress via dashboard
- **What's included:** Platform access, onboarding support, kickoff webinar, business getting started guide, email templates, aggregate reporting
- **Cost:** [Tier and pricing, customised per council]
- **Timeline:** Program can launch within 2 weeks of signing

**Design:** Professional, single page, Access Compass + council branding. Include 1-2 key stats (e.g., "850+ expert-designed questions," "35+ assessment areas," "[X] councils already participating").
