# Access Compass Growth Strategy Bible

**Created**: 11 April 2026
**Goal**: $1M+ ARR within 24 months. $3M+ ARR within 36 months.
**Mantra**: Package the program. Close CoM. Prove it works. Scale councils. Go international.

## THE CORE MODEL

**Stop thinking:** "Sell the platform + add consulting"
**Start thinking:** "Sell a program, powered by Access Compass"

Your business has two arms:

| Arm | Role | Revenue Timeline |
|-----|------|-----------------|
| **Consulting + Facilitation** | Front-end revenue. Gets you in the room. Builds trust. Drives engagement. | Now (months 1-6 primary revenue) |
| **Access Compass Platform** | The engine. Makes programs scalable, trackable, repeatable. Enables you to serve 50 businesses instead of 5. | Growing (months 3-12 takes over as primary) |

Consulting leads for 6 months while the platform proves itself. Then the platform takes over. This transition is deliberate and conscious. Don't get stuck in the consulting trap where revenue scales linearly with your time.

## FIRST 90 DAYS: 3 OUTCOMES

Everything in the next 90 days serves exactly three outcomes. If an activity doesn't serve one of these, it waits.

### Outcome 1: Be able to take payment and onboard cleanly

You cannot sell what people can't buy. Stripe, terms, pricing page, onboarding assets.

### Outcome 2: Close one strong pilot or program customer

City of Melbourne is the target. One warm council, one additional if possible. Sell a program, not a platform.

### Outcome 3: Turn that customer into a case study and repeatable sales narrative

Document everything. Completion rates, business feedback, council outcomes. This case study opens every door for the next 2 years.

**Everything else is secondary.**

## THE COUNCIL PROGRAM OFFER

This is the single most important thing to get right. When a council asks "what are we buying?", the answer must be instant and clear.

### Access Compass Council Program

**What it is:** A structured accessibility program for local businesses, powered by the Access Compass platform.

**What businesses experience:**
- Expert-guided self-assessment of their venue, services, and operations
- Practical resources showing how to fix what they find
- A prioritised action plan they can work through at their own pace
- Progress tracking and re-assessment to show improvement

**What the council gets:**
- Authority dashboard with real-time enrolment, progress, and completion data
- Aggregate reporting for DIAP and community impact reporting
- Program support: onboarding webinar, business outreach templates, getting started guide
- Optional facilitation layer for higher engagement

**Three delivery options (for sales conversations, not pricing page):**

| Option | What's Included | Indicative Price |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Light Program** | Platform access, templates, webinar, reporting | $5,000-$10,000 |
| **Guided Program** | Everything above + 2 workshops, monthly check-ins, completion report | $10,000-$20,000 |
| **Full Program** | Everything above + individual business support, council DIAP advisory, quarterly strategy | $25,000-$50,000 |

Price varies by: number of businesses, duration, council size, facilitation depth. Always scope before quoting.

**Founding partner rate:** 30-40% off for the first 3-5 councils. The case studies are worth more than the discount.

**On the pricing page:** Show the platform tiers (Essentials/Standard/Pro) with "Program support" features listed. Below the tiers, one line: "Need hands-on support? We offer program facilitation, workshops, and advisory services to help councils maximise engagement and outcomes. [Talk to us]"

## CRITICAL ASSUMPTIONS

This plan depends on the following being true. Test them early. If any prove false, adjust.

1. **Councils will buy a program-led software model.** They're used to buying consultant time or software licenses separately. A bundled "program powered by platform" is a new category. If early councils resist, you may need to sell facilitation first and introduce the platform as the delivery mechanism.

2. **Businesses will complete assessments with limited support.** Self-service completion rates are lower than consultant-led. If completion drops below 40%, the value proposition to councils weakens. Mitigation: onboarding assets, facilitation add-on, council engagement.

3. **Pilot participants can be converted into proof stories.** Case studies require: measurable outcomes, willing participants, enough time to show results. If the first pilot is too small or too short, the case study won't be compelling.

4. **Your current network is strong enough to open the first 5-10 conversations.** If warm outreach yields fewer than 3 real conversations in 6 weeks, you need to invest in cold outreach or channel partnerships sooner.

5. **Onboarding can be made light enough to scale without you doing everything manually.** If every council needs 20+ hours of your time to launch, facilitation revenue won't cover the cost at lower price points. The onboarding assets must actually reduce your time per council.

6. **The product is stable enough for paying customers to have a good experience.** If the first council's businesses hit bugs, sync issues, or confusing UX, the case study becomes a cautionary tale. Product stability is a prerequisite, not a nice-to-have.

## PARTNER APPROACH PLAYBOOK

### How to Approach Councils

**Channel:** Email to accessibility officer, community development manager, or tourism/events manager. LinkedIn as backup.

**Timing:** April-June (budget planning for July FY), September-October (mid-year allocation).

**Positioning:** "A structured accessibility program for your local businesses, with built-in tracking and reporting." NOT "a tool."

**Follow-up cadence:** Day 1 (email) -> Day 7 (follow-up) -> Day 14 (LinkedIn) -> Day 21 (phone) -> Day 30 (final email + webinar invite). After 5 touches, move to nurture list.

**Demo structure (30 min):**
- 5 min: Their goals and challenges
- 10 min: Platform demo (business experience, authority dashboard, reporting)
- 5 min: Program support and facilitation options
- 5 min: Pricing (present 2 options: light vs guided program)
- 5 min: Next steps

### How to Approach Associations

**Channel:** Email to CEO or membership/partnerships manager.

**Timing:** 2-3 months before their annual conference or member event.

**Pitch:** "Co-branded accessibility program for your members. They get a discount, you get visibility as an accessibility leader, we handle everything."

**Deal structures:** Referral fee ($50-$100/signup), free dashboard, revenue share (10-15%), or sponsorship fee.

### How to Approach Insurers

**Channel:** LinkedIn to Head of ESG, Partnerships, or Innovation. NOT sales.

**Timing:** After 50+ businesses on platform.

**Pitch:** "Only structured accessibility compliance dataset in Australia. Explore co-branded offering or risk signal integration."

**Expect:** 6-12 month exploration. Multiple stakeholders. Pilot first. Then scale.

### How to Approach State Government

**Channel:** Warm intro through council contacts, or direct to innovation/grants team.

**Timing:** After 5+ council case studies.

**Pitch:** "Proven at LGA level with X councils. Here's state-wide deployment." Offer a regional pilot (5 councils, 6 months).
