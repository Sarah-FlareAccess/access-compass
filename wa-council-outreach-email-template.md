# WA Council Outreach. Email Templates

For use against `wa-councils-outreach-list.csv` (137 councils). WA councils use **DAIP** (Disability Access and Inclusion Plan), same as SA, different from VIC/NSW (DIAP).

Sent from sarah@flareaccess.com.

**Context (for your reference, not for the email body):** WA's Disability Services Act 1993 s.28 statutorily requires every WA local government to publish a DAIP and review it every 5 years, with annual progress reporting to the Department of Communities. State strategy is "A Western Australia for Everyone" 2020–2030. WA was the first state to legislate council DAIPs (1993), 30+ years of compliance culture. **Do NOT lecture them about any of this in the email.** They live it.

**Tone rule (do not break):** Lead with the council's own commitment or specific work. You are a stranger; they know their statutory obligations better than you do. See `feedback-outreach-email-style.md` in memory.

- [ ] Council name spelled correctly with full WA naming (City of / Shire of / Town of)
- [ ] First name only if confirmed on website. Default to "Community Development team"
- [ ] Opener block matches what THIS council has actually done (pick A/B/C/D/E/F/G)
- [ ] No lecture about Disability Services Act 1993 / annual reporting / Department of Communities lodgement
- [ ] State strategy referenced as their commitment ("your commitment under...") not as a mandate
- [ ] DAIP spelled as "DAIP" (matches WA + SA convention)
- [ ] Both products named where relevant (Access Compass + Authority Portal)
- [ ] Concrete department list, not abstract "facility assessments"

## Notes for batch sending

- Stagger across Tue / Wed / Thu mornings, WA time (AEST minus 2 hr standard, minus 3 hr DST)
- Don't BCC. Send individually with personalisation
- Track replies in `wa-councils-outreach-list.csv` (Status / DateSent / NextActionDate columns)
- For Pilbara / Kimberley councils: avoid Friday morning sends (often miss the responsive window before remote-area weekends); prefer Tue–Wed
- For councils with named contacts, address directly. For "team only" councils, use "Hi Community Development team"
- Avoid WA school holidays + WALGA conference weeks; check before each batch
- Plan to revisit cadence once Mornington Peninsula case study is published, adds credibility for Perth metro batch
