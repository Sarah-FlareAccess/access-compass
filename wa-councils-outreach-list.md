# Western Australian Local Councils — DAIP Outreach List

**Total LGAs covered:** 137 of 137 (the 137 WA local government areas under the Local Government Act 1995. Christmas Island and Cocos (Keeling) Islands are Commonwealth territories administered as WA-style shires and are listed separately at the foot of the file. Joint regional councils such as Pilbara Regional Council and the Wheatbelt regional councils are noted in the cross-reference appendix, not the main tier tables.)
**Date generated:** 2026-05-14
**Maintained by:** Access Compass outreach
**Statutory anchor:** WA Disability Services Act 1993 s.28 requires every local government to publish a DAIP and review it on a 5-year cycle, with annual progress reporting to the Department of Communities. Strongest mandate in Australia.

## How to use
- Work top-down: Tier 1 (live consultations, recently adopted plans, plus all Pilbara/Kimberley councils flagged for high-budget priority regardless of plan status) get warm outreach this week and next. Tier 2 (cycle-due — plan period 2020–2024 or 2021–2025/2026 expiring this cycle) is the second batch with "your DAIP refresh is due" framing. Tier 3 is the broad WA wave once Mornington case study lands.
- Where the named officer column says "team only" or "not found", use the general council email and ask in the opening line for the role title (Community Development / Inclusion Officer / Community Wellbeing Manager). Do not invent names.
- Region terminology follows the WA Development Commission boundaries (Perth Metro, Peel, South West, Great Southern, Wheatbelt, Mid West, Gascoyne, Pilbara, Kimberley, Goldfields-Esperance).

## Cross-reference: regional groupings already noted

- **Wheatbelt regional voluntary groupings:** WA's Wheatbelt has multiple voluntary regional groupings (Avon Subregional Economic Strategy, Central Country Zone, North Eastern Wheatbelt Regional Organisation of Councils — NEWROC, Wheatbelt East Regional Organisation of Councils — WEROC, Wheatbelt North Sub Regional Organisation of Councils — WNSROC, Northern Country Zone). DAIP work is generally LGA-by-LGA, not joint, but smaller shires sometimes share Community Development Officers across NEWROC/WEROC. Worth asking when reaching small shires whether they share a DAIP framework.
- **Pilbara Regional Council:** Voluntary regional council comprising Karratha, Port Hedland, Ashburton, East Pilbara. Not a DAIP author itself but coordinates regional advocacy. Worth a single warm contact in case they want to broker a Pilbara-wide conversation.
- **Kimberley Regional Group:** Voluntary regional grouping of Broome, Derby–West Kimberley, Halls Creek, Wyndham–East Kimberley. Recent meeting minutes February 2026 show active coordination on shared priorities. Same play as Pilbara — regional broker potential.
- **South West Group:** Voluntary regional council comprising Bunbury, Capel, Collie, Dardanup, Donnybrook–Balingup, Harvey, and the City of Busselton (Greater Bunbury sub-region). Coordinates economic development and shared services.
- **Goldfields Voluntary Regional Organisation of Councils (GVROC):** Coolgardie, Dundas, Esperance, Kalgoorlie–Boulder, Laverton, Leonora, Menzies, Ngaanyatjarraku, Ravensthorpe, Wiluna. Coordinates Goldfields-Esperance regional priorities.
- **Forum of Regional Councils of WA (FORWARD):** Peak forum for the voluntary regional council bodies.
- **Metropolitan regional councils** (waste/services, not DAIP): Eastern Metropolitan Regional Council, Mindarie Regional Council, Rivers Regional Council, Tamala Park Regional Council, Western Metropolitan Regional Council. Out of scope for DAIP outreach.
